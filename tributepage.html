General Information

Albert Einstein was a German-born theoretical physicist, widely considered one of the greatest and most influential scientists of all time. Although best known for developing the theory of relativity, he also made important contributions to quantum mechanics. Einstein received the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics for the discovery of the photoelectric effect.

Albert Einstein's timeline

  • 1879 - Albert Einstein is born in Ulm, German Empire.
  • 1896 - Enrolls in a four-year mathematics and physics program at the Federal polytechnic school in Zürich.
  • 1900 - Receives his Federal teaching diploma after completing the mathematics and physics program.
  • 1903 - Marries his first wife, Mileva Marić, who he met while completing his college diploma.
  • 1905 - Publishes 4 scientific papers pertaining to the photoelectric effect, Brownian motion, special relativity and matter-energy equivalence (the famous E=mc2 equation was created here).
  • 1907-1915 - Developes the General relativity theory, wich completed his previous work on Special relativity. Predicted the phenomena of gravitational time dilation, gravitational waves, gravitational redshift and deflection of light.
  • 1919 - After divorcing his first wife, Einstein marries Elsa Löwenthal, his first cousin maternally, and second cousin paternally, who dies in 1936 after being diagnosed with heart and kidney problems a year earlier.
  • 1921-1922 - Starts a series of travel, wich took him to the US, England, Singapore, Ceylon (now Sri Lanka), Japan, Palestine and Spain. Gives several public lectures and visites renowned universities.
  • 1922 - Receives the 1921 Nobel Prize in physics for his services to Theoretical Physics, and specially fos his discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect.
  • 1925 - Visits South America. Spends a month in Argenina, a week in Uruguay and a week in Brazil.
  • 1930-1931 - Visits the US for the second time. Albert was taken to multiple places and events, including Chinatown, a performance of Carmen at the Metropolitan Opera, Madison Square Garden during a Hanukkah celebration, and joined Charli Chaplin as his special guests during Chaplin's City Lights premiere.
  • 1933 - Einstein emigrates to the US after renouncing to his German citizenship when the Nazis rose to power under Adolf Hitler's direction.
  • 1935 - In collaboration with Natan Rosen, Einstein produces a model of a wormhole, also called "Einstein-Rosen bridges".
  • 1950 - Attemps to generalize his theory of gravitation to include electromagnetism as aspects of a single entity, a Unified field theory. Although unsuccessful, his work has motivated modern quests for a theory of everything, in particular string theory.
  • 1953 - Dies at the age of 76 on 17 april after experiencing internal bleeding caused by the rupture of an abdominal aortic aneurism. Refused surgery, saying: I want to go when I want. It is tasteless to prolong life artificially. I have done my share; it is time to go. I will do it elegantly.
